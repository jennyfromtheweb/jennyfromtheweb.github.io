Berkeley EECS → Software Engineer → UX Technologist → Product Manager → User Researcher

After graduating with a degree in Electrical Engineering and Computer Science at UC Berkeley, I started working as a software engineer — but I soon realized the part of building products I loved most was figuring out how to make them genuinely help people.

In search of the chance to shift my work from writing code to advocating for users, I became a UX Technologist (bridging engineering and design), then a Product Manager, before becoming one of the first User Researchers at Rubrik. With each role, I brought the voice of the user further into the conversation — whether that meant building empathy through persona research and journey mapping, feeding insights into product strategy, or facilitating design-thinking workshops.

In the enterprise world, I specialized in translating technical complexity into human-centered product strategy, launching zero-to-one initiatives in ambiguous problem spaces. Today, I'm channeling that expertise toward social impact — using digital strategy, systems thinking, and mixed-methods research to help mission-driven organizations tackle complex problems.

Based in San Francisco Bay Area
